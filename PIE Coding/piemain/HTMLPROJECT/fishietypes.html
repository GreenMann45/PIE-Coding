> Types of Fishies! <



There are many types of fish in the sea! We are going to take a look at a few types!

                  

The Shark



•Sharks do not have bones.
•Most sharks have good eyesight.
•Shark skin feels similar to sandpaper.
•Sharks have been around a very long time.
•Scientists age sharks by counting the rings on their vertebrae.
                  

The Stingray



•The largest species of stingray measure 6.5 feet in length and can weigh up to 790 pounds.
•Stingrays are closely related to sharks.
•Their long tails usually have a spine and venom.
•Stingrays eat clams, shrimp, and mussels.
•Stingrays don't use their eyes to find prey.
                  

The Seahorse




•Seahorses have a big appetite.
•They mate for life.
•The males hold babies.
•They use their tails as tools.
•They camouflage.
•Their eyes work independently of one another.
•They don't have that many predators.
                  

The Eel



•There are over 400 species of eel.
•Eels live in both saltwater and fresh water.
•Even though the eel looks like a snake it is really a fish.
•Eels have gills and very sharp teeth.
•Most eels hide and live in caves and rock crevices.
                  

The Fish Fish



•Fish are social.
•Fish are smart.
•Fish are excellent communicators.
•Fish are flexible.
•Fish can migrate extremely far distances.
•Fish have exceptional sensory capabilities.
•Fish are important to sustaining ecosystems.
